A bold Indonesian novel about four urban women and a former priest, Saman, exploring freedom, politics, and religion during the repressive New Order era, breaking taboos and giving voice to modern feminist ideas.

Darkly magical and satirical novel about Dewi Ayu, a beautiful prostitute who rises from her grave, and her family’s cursed life through colonialism, war, and political violence, mixing folklore, absurd humor, and sharp social critique to explore power, beauty, and Indonesia’s painful history.

Historical novel about Dimas Suryo, an Indonesian exile in Paris after the 1965 purge, and his daughter Lintang, who returns home to uncover his hidden past, blending personal and political histories to explore identity, exile, and the longing for home.

Classic Indonesian novel about Srintil, a village girl chosen to be a ronggeng dancer with mystical powers, whose life in 1960s Dukuh Paruk is shaped by community expectations and the political upheaval of the communist purge, telling a story of innocence, dignity, and Javanese culture
